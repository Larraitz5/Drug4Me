 Insulin requirements may be inc reased by medicinal products with hyperglycaemic activity, such as oral contraceptives, corticoster oids, or thyroid replacement therapy, danazol, beta 2 stimulants (such as ritodrine, salbutam ol, terbutaline).  
 Insulin requirements may be reduced in the pr esence of medicinal products with hypoglycaemic activity, such as oral hypoglycaemics, salicylates (for example, acetylsalicylic acid), sulpha antibiotics, certain antidepressants (monoamine oxidase inhibitors , selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors ), cer tain angiotensin converting enzyme inhibitors (captopril, enalapril), angiotensin II receptor block ers, beta -blockers, octreotide or alcohol.
